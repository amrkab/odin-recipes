French Toast

Description

French toast is a classic and comforting breakfast dish enjoyed by many. To make it, you'll need slices of bread, preferably a day or two old, which are soaked in a mixture of beaten eggs, milk, and a dash of vanilla extract for added flavor. After a brief dip in this custard-like mixture, the bread is pan-fried in butter until it's golden brown and crispy on the outside, while still soft and slightly custardy on the inside. This delightful treat can be customized to your taste, topped with a variety of options like maple syrup, powdered sugar, fresh fruits, or even a dollop of whipped cream. It's a simple yet satisfying morning indulgence that brings warmth and nostalgia to your breakfast table.

Ingredients

  • Slices of Bread (2-4 slices)
  • Eggs (2-4)
  • Milk 1/2 cup
  • Vanilla Extract 1 teaspoon
  • Crushed Red Pepper Flakes
  • Butter (for frying)

Steps

  1. Prepare the Egg Mixture:
    1. Crack the eggs into a mixing bowl.
    2. Add milk and vanilla extract to the eggs.
    3. Whisk the mixture until it's well combined.
  2. Soak the Bread:
    1. Heat a non-stick skillet or griddle over medium heat and add a small amount of butter to coat the surface.
    2. Take a slice of bread and dip it into the egg mixture. Make sure to coat both sides evenly but don't soak it too long, as you don't want the bread to become too soggy.
  3. Cook the French Toast:
    1. Place the soaked bread slice on the heated skillet or griddle.
    2. Cook each side until it's golden brown, usually about 2-3 minutes per side.
    3. Continue this process for each slice of bread, adding more butter to the pan as needed.
  4. Serve:
    1. Once the French toast is cooked to your desired level of crispiness, remove it from the pan.
    2. Serve hot and top with your choice of toppings like maple syrup, powdered sugar, fresh fruit, or whipped cream.
